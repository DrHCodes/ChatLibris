# 60-second demo plan

## Query one: answerable

> Does creatine supplementation improve strength in healthy adults?

Narration:

> Most AI systems answer even when reliable evidence is missing. ChatLibris starts by searching an academic paper index. The synthesis model receives only the retrieved paper abstracts—not Reddit, blogs, or ordinary search results. Every key finding points to a paper, and the app shows the limitations alongside the answer.

Show:

1. The question being submitted
2. The academic-search progress states
3. The verdict and confidence
4. Claim-level `P1`, `P2` citations
5. The direct-evidence labels on source cards

## Query two: abstention

> Does sparkling water improve software debugging accuracy?

Narration:

> When the retrieved research cannot establish an answer, ChatLibris does not fill the gap with assumptions. It says unknown. Unknown does not mean false; it means the answer was not established by the literature searched.

## Closing line

> ChatLibris: ask the literature, get evidence—not vibes.
